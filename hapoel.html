Hapoel Jerusalem: Data to Graphs

The Head Coach of the Hapoel Jerusalem Soccer Team has hours and hours of game footage to scour through and needs to identify trends that lead to team success. My boss, the Head of Data Analytics, tasked me with summarizing all the data into the game's most crucial moments; an exercise in moving from data processing to data visualization.

I was provided with a sample XML file, unique to a particular game, consisting of all the data from every single play, including the smallest details of who passed the ball to whom, where on the field, and at what time of the game. I created a Python script that parsed this data, stored it all in a dataset, and converted it into graphs depicting all the team's shots on goal, as seen in the diagrams below. My experience with string and array manipulation helped me organize the data for my needs.

I color coded each line on the graph to reflect a dribble, pass, and shot, respectively, created a legend that documented the chronology of plays within the possession, and added a title that described the moment, including who shot the ball, the time elapsed, and the result of the play. Now, the head coach can upload an XML file to my script and have all the important moments of the game to analyze within seconds. He can then identify trends and patterns for team success and make decisions more efficiently.
